# IRS releases tax inflation adjustments for tax year 2026, including amendments from the One, Big, Beautiful Bill

IR-2025-103, Oct. 9, 2025

WASHINGTON — The Internal Revenue Service today announced the tax year 2026 annual inflation adjustments for more than 60 tax provisions, including the tax rate schedules and other tax changes. [Revenue Procedure 2025-32 **PDF**](https://www.irs.gov/pub/irs-drop/rp-25-32.pdf) provides details about these annual adjustments.

## Notable changes under the One, Big, Beautiful Bill

The tax year 2026 adjustments described below generally apply to tax returns filed in 2027. The OBBBA made permanent most TCJA individual tax provisions and introduced additional inflation adjustments for the bottom two brackets (10% and 12%) at 4%, versus ~2.3% for the higher brackets. On average, tax parameters adjusted for inflation increased by about 2.7 percent.

- **Standard deductions.** For tax year 2026, the standard deduction increases to $32,200 for married couples filing jointly. For single taxpayers and married individuals filing separately, the standard deduction rises to $16,100 for tax year 2026, and for heads of households, the standard deduction will be $24,150.

  | Standard deduction | Single / Married Filing Separately | Married Filing Jointly | Heads of Households |
  | --- | --- | --- | --- |
  | TY 2025 Under OBBBA | $15,750 | $31,500 | $23,625 |
  | TY 2026 Under OBBBA | $16,100 | $32,200 | $24,150 |

- **Marginal rates.** For tax year 2026, the top tax rate remains 37% for individual single taxpayers with incomes greater than $640,600 ($768,700 for married couples filing jointly). The other rates are:

  - 35% for incomes over $256,225 ($512,450 for married couples filing jointly).
  - 32% for incomes over $201,775 ($403,550 for married couples filing jointly).
  - 24% for incomes over $105,700 ($211,400 for married couples filing jointly).
  - 22% for incomes over $50,400 ($100,800 for married couples filing jointly).
  - 12% for incomes over $12,400 ($24,800 for married couples filing jointly).
  - 10% for incomes of $12,400 or less ($24,800 or less for married couples filing jointly).

  Full bracket table per [Tax Foundation summary of Rev. Proc. 2025-32](https://taxfoundation.org/data/all/federal/2026-tax-brackets/):

  | Tax Rate | Single | Married Filing Jointly | Heads of Household | Married Filing Separately |
  | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
  | 10% | $0 – $12,400 | $0 – $24,800 | $0 – $17,700 | $0 – $12,400 |
  | 12% | $12,401 – $50,400 | $24,801 – $100,800 | $17,701 – $67,450 | $12,401 – $50,400 |
  | 22% | $50,401 – $105,700 | $100,801 – $211,400 | $67,451 – $105,700 | $50,401 – $105,700 |
  | 24% | $105,701 – $201,775 | $211,401 – $403,550 | $105,701 – $201,775 | $105,701 – $201,775 |
  | 32% | $201,776 – $256,225 | $403,551 – $512,450 | $201,776 – $256,200 | $201,776 – $256,225 |
  | 35% | $256,226 – $640,600 | $512,451 – $768,700 | $256,201 – $640,600 | $256,226 – $384,350 |

  > **Note on Married Filing Separately 35% top:** The MFS 35% ceiling ($384,350) is derived as half of the MFJ 37% threshold ($768,700), consistent with the statutory relationship applied in prior years.

- **Alternative minimum tax exemption amounts.** For tax year 2026, the exemption amount for unmarried individuals is $90,100 and begins to phase out at $500,000. For married couples filing jointly, the exemption amount is $140,200 and begins to phase out at $1,000,000. The OBBBA changed the phaseout rate from 25% to 50 cents per dollar earned.

- **Earned income tax credits.** For qualifying taxpayers who have three or more qualifying children, the tax year 2026 maximum Earned Income Tax Credit amount is $8,231, an increase from $8,046 for tax year 2025.

- **Qualified transportation fringe benefit.** For tax year 2026, the monthly limitation for the qualified transportation fringe benefit and the monthly limitation for qualified parking increases to $340, up $15 from 2025.

- **Health flexible spending cafeteria plans.** For tax years beginning in 2026, the dollar limitation for voluntary employee salary reductions for contributions to health flexible spending arrangements increases to $3,400, up $100 from 2025. For cafeteria plans that permit the carryover of unused amounts, the maximum carryover amount is $680, an increase of $20 from 2025.

- **Foreign earned income exclusion.** For tax year 2026, the foreign earned income exclusion is $132,900, up from $130,000 for tax year 2025.

- **Estate tax credits.** Estates of decedents who die during 2026 have a basic exclusion amount of $15,000,000, up from $13,990,000 for estates of decedents who died in 2025.

- **Annual exclusion for gifts** remains at $19,000 for calendar year 2026. The annual exclusion for gifts to a spouse who is not a citizen of the United States increases to $194,000, up $4,000 from calendar year 2025.

- **Adoption credits.** The maximum credit allowed for adoptions for tax year 2026 is the amount of qualified adoption expenses up to $17,670, up from $17,280 for 2025. The refundable portion is $5,120.

## FICA / Social Security wage base for 2026

Per the Social Security Administration ([SSA COLA Fact Sheet 2026](https://www.ssa.gov/news/en/cola/factsheets/2026.html) and [SSA press release Oct. 24, 2025](https://www.ssa.gov/news/en/press/releases/2025-10-24.html)):

- **Social Security wage base:** $184,500 (up from $176,100 for 2025)
- **Social Security tax rate:** 6.2% for employees; 6.2% for employers (12.4% combined for self-employed)
- **Medicare tax rate:** 1.45% for employees and employers each (2.9% combined for self-employed)
- **Additional Medicare tax:** 0.9% on wages/SE income above $200,000 (single), $250,000 (married filing jointly), $125,000 (married filing separately) — **thresholds are not adjusted for inflation**

## Unchanged for tax year 2026

- **Personal exemptions** remain at $0. The elimination of the personal exemption was made permanent by the OBBBA.
- **Itemized deductions.** The limitation on itemized deductions was eliminated by the TCJA and made permanent by the OBBBA (with a limitation on the tax benefit from itemized deductions for the 37% bracket).
- **Lifetime learning credits.** The MAGI phaseout thresholds ($80,000–$90,000 single; $160,000–$180,000 joint) are not adjusted for inflation for taxable years beginning after Dec. 31, 2020.
- **Additional Medicare tax thresholds** ($200,000 / $250,000 / $125,000) are set by statute and are not inflation-adjusted.
